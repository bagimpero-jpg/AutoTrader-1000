# Concept: Range Trading & Market Rotation

## Definition
The market spends the majority of its time in consolidation (Accumulation). Ranges form after large impulse moves as the market enters a "cool-down" phase. Trade ONLY the extremes — never the middle.

## The Three Range Trading Models

### Model 1: The Deviation (A+ Setup)
- Price sweeps above Range High (or below Range Low) and fails to sustain.
- Price re-enters range and forms LTF Breaker Block.
- In Bullish trend: prioritize Range Low Sweeps (sweep bottom → go to top).
- `IF (Price Sweeps Range boundary) AND (LTF Breaker forms inside range) THEN Enter(Target = Opposite boundary)`

### Model 2: Expansion Breakdown (Bearish)
- Price breaks below range and STAYS there (no immediate re-entry).
- Wait for "Second Break" — price retests broken range low (now Supply Zone) and breaks new low.
- Prevents fakeout traps. Confirms range is over and new trend has begun.

### Model 3: Expansion Breakout (Bullish)
- Mirror of Model 2. Price breaks above range and holds.
- Wait for retest of broken range high (now Demand Zone) and second structural break upside.

## The 5-Step Execution Protocol
1. **Range Identification**: Mark boundary high/low after impulse move.
2. **Manipulation/Deviation**: Wait for fakeout/sweep of one side.
3. **Market Structure Shift**: Detect CHoCH or Breaker on LTF (1m/5m).
4. **POI Retest**: Enter on retest of reversal candle (OB or S/D zone).
5. **Objective Targeting**: Target opposite range boundary or nearest HTF liquidity.

## Marking Rules
- Top: Highest Wick to Highest Body Close.
- Bottom: Lowest Wick to Lowest Body Close.
- **50% Rule (Mid-Range)**: Equilibrium acts as rejection/bounce. Use as TP1 or Break Even zone.
- **One-Time Use**: Once market rotates from Point A to Point B, that range cycle is "Filled."

## Entry Rules
- Only trade the deviation/sweep extremes.
- Require LTF Breaker Block confirmation.
- Do NOT loop-trade the same range — breakout probability increases with each tap.

## Exit Rules
- TP at opposite range boundary.
- Alternative: nearest HTF External Liquidity.

## Session Context
- Ranges typically form during Asian session.
- Deviation/sweep during London open.
- Expansion during NY session.
