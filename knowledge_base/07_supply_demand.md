# Concept: Supply & Demand Zones & Full Stack Execution

## Definition
Unlike Order Blocks (single candles), Supply & Demand zones are entire consolidation areas before an impulsive move. They represent larger accumulations of institutional orders and are higher probability than single-candle OBs.

## Identification Rules
- **Demand Zone**: Entire sideways consolidation range BEFORE price pumps and breaks a High.
- **Supply Zone**: Entire sideways consolidation range BEFORE price dumps and breaks a Low.
- **Probability Filter**: Zone with multiple consolidation candles > single-candle OB (more institutional order accumulation).

## The Full Stack Execution Logic (Every Trade)
### Step 1: HTF Bias (The Anchor)
- Identify trend on Weekly/Daily.
- ONLY take trades in HTF trend direction (Daily Bullish → only Buy setups).

### Step 2: POI Identification (The Zone)
- Find untested level on Daily or 4H:
  1. HTF FVG
  2. HTF Order Block
  3. HTF Supply/Demand Consolidation
- **Overlapping levels (FVG inside Demand Zone) = High-Probability Level.**

### Step 3: LTF Trigger (The Entry)
- Price touches HTF level → switch to 5m (or 1m for scalps).
- Wait for Breaker Block on LTF.
- Enter on BB retest.

### Step 4: Liquidity Targeting (The Exit)
- TP at nearest HTF External Liquidity (Old Highs/Lows or EQH/EQL).
- SL behind LTF Breaker Block or most recent structural swing.

## Entry Rules
- Must follow all 4 steps sequentially. Skip none.
- "Trade Ready" state ONLY when price enters an aggregate zone AND LTF confirms via Breaker Block.
- Professional limit: 2-3 trades per day maximum.

## Exit Rules
- Minimum 1:2 RR, ideal 1:3 RR.
- Ensures profitability even with 40% win rate.

## Fundamental Overrides
- War/Uncertainty: Bullish for Gold, Bearish for paper currencies.
- Monday opens / major news: Market often traps retail before reversing into HTF direction. Wait for sweep before engaging.

## Session Context
- HTF levels mapped during Asian/early London.
- Execution during London and NY only.
