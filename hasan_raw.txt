I. Candlestick Mechanics (Data Ingestion)
The bot must parse OHLC (Open, High, Low, Close) data to determine the "State" of the market:
● Bullish Candle: Close > Open. Represents buying pressure.
● Bearish Candle: Close < Open. Represents selling pressure.
● Wick Analysis: The High and Low points of a candle are "Liquidity Points." The bot
must track these for "Sweeps."
II. Market States (Directional Bias)
The bot will categorize the market into one of three states at any given time:
1. Bullish: Breaking Highs, Protecting Lows.
2. Bearish: Breaking Lows, Protecting Highs.
3. Sideways (Range): No clear break of structural Highs/Lows. (Bot should ideally stay in
"Wait Mode" or use "Range Scaling" logic).
III. Structural Validation Rules (The "Anti-Trap" Logic)
This is the most critical logic for preventing the bot from taking "Fake" trades:
1. The Confirmation Rule (High/Low Validation)
● New Higher Low (HL): A low point is NOT a valid "Protected Low" until the market
successfully breaks and closes above the previous Higher High (HH).
--- PAGE 4 ---
● New Lower High (LH): A high point is NOT a valid "Protected High" until the market
successfully breaks and closes below the previous Lower Low (LL).
● Bot Logic: If the bot identifies a "Low" but price has not yet broken the "High," it must
flag this as Internal Structure and avoid placing a Stop Loss there.
2. Protected vs. Targeted Levels
● Protected Levels: The validated HL (in an uptrend) or LH (in a downtrend). If price hits
these, the trend is considered "Shifted."
● Targeted Levels: The weak HH or LL that the market is expected to run through.
IV. Trend Shift Mechanics (BOS & CHoCH)
● Break of Structure (BOS): When price continues the trend by breaking a validated High
(Bullish) or Low (Bearish).
● Change of Character (CHoCH): The first time a "Protected Level" is broken.
○ Example: In a Bullish trend, if price closes below the most recent Validated Low,
the bot must immediately flip its bias to Bearish.
V. Execution Guardrails (Stop Loss Placement)
● Rule: The SL must always be placed behind a Validated Protected Level.
● Refinement: If the bot enters a trade before a new HL/LH is validated, it is "Gambling on
Internal Structure." The bot must wait for the Break of Structure (BOS) to confirm the
entry is safe.

An FVG (or Imbalance) is a 3-candle pattern that
indicates a sudden surge in buying or selling
pressure, leaving behind a "gap" where price was
not efficiently delivered.
● Bullish FVG (Buy Setup):
○ The Rule: Mark the High of Candle 1 and
the Low of Candle 3.
○ If there is an empty space (gap) between
Candle 1's High and Candle 3's Low, that is
a Bullish FVG.
○ Note: Candle color and wick length do not
change the rule; only the price gap matters.
● Bearish FVG (Sell Setup):
--- PAGE 10 ---
○ The Rule: Mark the Low of Candle 1 and
the High of Candle 3.
○ If there is a gap between them, that is a
Bearish FVG.
II. Strategic Logic (Internal vs. External Liquidity)
The bot treats the FVG as a "magnetic" pending
order zone.
● The Flow (Market Rotation):
○ FVG = Internal Range Liquidity.
○ Old Highs/Lows = External Range
Liquidity.
○ Bot Logic: Once price taps into an FVG
(Internal), the bot anticipates a move toward
the next major High or Low (External) as the
target (TP).
● Invalidation:
○ An FVG is considered "failed" or invalid if a
candle closes completely outside the gap.
○ One-Time Use: An FVG should generally
be used for its First Tap only. Subsequent
taps have a higher risk of failing.
